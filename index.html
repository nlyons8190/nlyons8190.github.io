About the project

This project began by examining data related to American emissions. I think that often issues related to emissions are framed in such a way that the culprit is the petroleum industy. I wanted to use this project to explore the emission problem in a more open framework, understanding that many facets of American society contribute to the problem.

The original dataset was focused on facility-level emissions in the United States. Another dataset focused on global carbon emissions was used to give global context. Additional research was conducted to support the analysis of these two datasets.

Global Emissions Takeaway

The United States is one of the highest emission producers globally. Adjusting emission production by population shows that per capita emissions are drastically higher than other large emission producers. Technology is often seen as a solution to emission problems, but the United States is one of the most technologically advanced countries in the world. Why are emissions so prevalent in American society?

Where are these emissions coming from?

Surprising Leading Sources of Emissions

The largest producers in the United States are producers of electricity and heating. The United States Government is regularly one of the largest sources of emissions, which is interesting as they are a key player in emission regulation.

Conclusion

The United States is a leading producer of emissions, even with efforts to promote cleaner energy sources. The culture of comfort and convenience in the United States could be a driving force behind this issue. The adoption of sustainable technologies may not be sufficient, sustainable habits regarding consumption are needed to address this issue.
